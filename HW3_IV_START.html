Introduction

In this assignment, you will replicate the instrumental variable analysis from Stokes (2015), which examined how local wind turbine projects influenced electoral outcomes. Building on the matched dataset from Assignment 2, we will use Two-Stage Least Squares (2SLS) to estimate the causal effect of having a wind turbine proposed nearby on the change in Liberal Party vote share between 2007 and 2011. The instrument used in Stokes (2015) is a measure of local wind resource (average wind power, logged), which predicts where wind turbines are proposed. By using this instrument, we aim to isolate the portion of variation in turbine placement that is as-good-as-random, helping to meet the assumptions for causal identification.

Part 3: IV Assumptions and Validity

Evaluate Instrument Validity

Question 3: List the four key assumptions required for the IV strategy (2SLS) to identify a causal effect, and briefly explain what each one means in the context of this study. (Hint: think about what conditions a valid instrument must satisfy (relevance, exclusion,â€¦)

Response: Consider that Z is wind power potential and D is turbine placement and Y is vote share. This case study uses wind power as its instrumental variable. This variable satisfies the following four key assumptions: 1) Exclusion restriction: Wind power (Z) solely influences vote share (Y) through turbine placement (D) 2) Relevance: As shown in the first_stage f-statistic, windpower (Z) strongly influences turbine placement, this is known as relevance. 3) Ignorability (random first stage): Wind power is a random process that is determined through physical processes, not external political factors. 4) Monotonicity: As shown with the second_stage model, precincts that had strong wind power strongly influenced proposed wind turbines. This satisfies the montonomicity assumption meaning that there were no precincts less likely to get turbines when windpower is higher.

Robustness checking strategies utilized in Stokes, 2015

Question 4: Choose two robustness checks from the paper that the authors use to increase confidence in their causal identification strategy. For each one, summarize the logic and findings from the robustness check in your own words:

Response: The author uses the instrumental variable as a robustness check to address selection bias. The IV is used in order to address endogeneity concerns or to address the fact that turbine placement is not random, but also determined by windpower which in itself, is separate from political factors. If the regression analysis shows that there is a relationship between windpower and turbine placement, then there is not external bias. Another robustness check the author utilized was within the proximity to wind turbines. The author looked at different distances to wind turbines to test whether the effect was proximity-based. This led them to use 3km as the preferred buffer.
